PHYSICS DATABANKS

1. Motion & Speed

  • Speed: How fast an object moves (Distance ÷ Time).
  • Velocity: Speed WITH a specific direction (e.g., 50 m/s North).
  • Acceleration: A change in velocity (speeding up, slowing down, or turning). Measured in m/s².
  • Reference Point: A stationary object used to tell if something else is moving.

2. Forces & Friction

  • Force: A push or pull (measured in Newtons, N).
  • Net Force: When forces are balanced (Net Force = 0), objects don't change motion. Unbalanced forces cause acceleration.
  • Friction: A force opposing motion. Types include Static (not moving), Sliding, Rolling, and Fluid (air/water resistance).

3. Newton's 3 Laws of Motion

  • 1st Law (Inertia): An object stays at rest or in motion unless a force acts on it. (More mass = more inertia).
  • 2nd Law (F = ma): Force equals Mass times Acceleration. (Heavier objects need more force to accelerate).
  • 3rd Law (Action/Reaction): For every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction force.

4. Energy & Work

  • Kinetic Energy: Energy of motion.
  • Potential Energy: Stored energy (like a stretched rubber band or an object held high up).
  • Work: Done when a force moves an object a certain distance (measured in Joules).
  • Power: How fast work is done (measured in Watts).
